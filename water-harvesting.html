Rainwater Harvesting Cisterns

Drought is a constant concern in Mexico, and it's often difficult getting  out to rural communities. Rainwater harvesting cisterns hold 12,000 liters apiece and generally fill up over the course of a rainy season. This is enough water to last an entire family throughout the year. 

We have worked with CEDESA to build over 1,500 cisterns that have the potential to hold over 18 million liters of potable water. These cisterns are largely built in tandem with the homes' matriarchs. They take great pride in their achievement, and rightly so. With proper maintenance, it could last for generations. This program has been great for our club in that it's helped us gain the trust of small communities who otherwise might not open up to us.
